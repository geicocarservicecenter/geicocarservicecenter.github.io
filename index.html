The Insurance Claim & Repair Journey

When a vehicle is damaged in an accident, the repair process typically follows a structured insurance workflow. While individual policy details vary, most claims move through reporting, inspection, estimating, authorization, and final repair stages.

Claim Reporting

Submit incident details, supporting documentation, and damage photos to initiate review.

Damage Assessment

An adjuster evaluates visible and structural damage to prepare a repair estimate.

Authorization & Repair

Repairs proceed once estimates are reviewed under applicable coverage terms.

Selecting a Repair Facility

Policyholders generally retain the right to choose their repair provider. Options may include recommended repair networks or independent body shops. Certification standards such as ASE or I-CAR may indicate technician qualification.

Policyholder Rights & Considerations

Insurance regulations in many jurisdictions protect consumer choice in repair facilities. Understanding deductible obligations, supplemental estimates, and total loss thresholds can help reduce delays during claim resolution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I choose my own repair shop?
In most U.S. states, insurers cannot require you to use a specific facility.

What if additional damage is discovered?
Repair facilities may submit supplemental estimates for review and approval.

How are payments issued?
Payments typically reflect approved repair costs minus applicable deductibles.
